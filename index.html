INDIAN VILLAGES

Villages are backbone of our country. They make ~70% of India's population and the percentage has been decreasing over years. Due to COVID, reverse migration is happening. Their development is essential for development of Nation and GDP growth.

Livelihoods

Two major livelihoods in Rural India are Farm and Non-farm livelihoods.

Farm Livelihoods

  • Agriculture
  • Horticulture
  • Dairy

Non-farm Livelihoods

  • Small Business
  • Hotel
  • Barber Shop
  • Puncture Shop
  • Tailoring
  • Handicrafts
  • Carpentry

Religious Institutions

Indian Religion cannot be separated from society. Belief in God and religion is very strong in Rural India. These religious centres(Temple, Mosque, Church) are the main hubs for festivals, marriages and other community gatherings in villages.

Autonomy

Village Autonomy(Gram Swaraj) was conceived by Mahatma Gandhi who always stressed that villages should be independent, govern themselves and solve their own problems. Central and State Governments enacted different policies towards Gram Swaraj. Scheme by Central Government addressing this idea is Gram Swaraj Abhiyan

Health

Health is one of the most important Human Development Indicator. Villages have Primary Health Centres that give health care to all villagers. Health Care Workers reach each villager's homes for health checkups etc., Also, there are Mobile Hospitals, which can be reached by calling 104 Helpline.

Education

Education of all levels from primary to higher should be available in villages itself. A Central Government Scheme Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan aims to educate all the children between ages 6 to 14 by providing free education.

Child Care

Children under 6 years of age, pregnants and lactating mothers are taken care of in Anganwadis including immunization, primary health care and health check-up under ICDS scheme.
